## Infrastructure

- **A VM you control**, sized for roughly 8 containers running together (the database, an API gateway, a real-time service, and a few supporting services). As a starting point, **4 vCPUs / 8 GB RAM / 50 GB disk** comfortably covers a single org's usage; scale up if you expect heavy concurrent usage or large file uploads.
- **Docker and Docker Compose** installed on that VM.
- **`openssl` and `curl`** available on the VM — used during setup to generate credentials and fetch a configuration value from Orchid's central project.
- **Outbound network access** from the VM to Orchid's central project (to fetch its public signing key — see below) and **inbound access** from wherever your Zeno frontend is served, on the port you expose.

## From Orchid

Before you start, confirm with your Orchid contact that:

1. **Your central project is using modern (ES256) JWT signing.** This is a one-time step Orchid performs on their side — you don't need to do anything for it, but your deployment can't verify sessions until it's done. See [Architecture](/self-hosting/architecture#how-authentication-works-across-the-split) for why this matters.
2. **You have your central project's JWKS URL** — a public endpoint your deployment will fetch during setup. Your Orchid contact provides this.

<Warning>
  Don't attempt setup before step 1 is confirmed — your deployment will fail to verify any session token, and every authenticated request will be rejected.
</Warning>

## What you'll generate yourself

During setup you'll generate several credentials locally with `openssl` — nothing needs to be requested from Orchid beyond the JWKS URL above. The full list and exact commands are in [Setup](/self-hosting/setup).
