---
title: "Connecting the Frontend"
description: "Point your Zeno UI at your self-hosted database"
---

Once your backend is connected to your self-hosted database (handled by Orchid using the connection string from [Setup](/self-hosting/setup)), any member of your organization can point the Zeno frontend at it from **Settings → Data Connection**.

<Info>
  There's currently no separate organization-admin role — any member of your organization can view and change this setting.
</Info>

## Fields

<Steps>
  <Step title="Endpoint URL">
    Enter the frontend endpoint URL `setup.sh` printed at the end of [Setup](/self-hosting/setup) — your self-hosted instance's base URL, serving REST, Realtime, Storage, and Functions behind that one address.
  </Step>
  <Step title="API Key">
    Enter the `ANON_KEY` value from the same output. This field is write-only — once saved, it won't be shown again, and leaving it blank on a later edit keeps the current key.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test Connection">
    Click **Test Connection** before saving. This confirms your Zeno frontend can actually reach your endpoint and authenticate against it. A status badge (**Connected**, **Connection failed**, or **Not tested yet**) reflects the result, along with a timestamp of the last test.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enabled">
    Toggle this on once you're ready to switch over. While off, your organization keeps using Orchid's default hosting regardless of the URL and key entered above — so you can save and test your configuration without affecting anyone until you're ready.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Save your configuration. From this point on, your organization's conversations, automations, dashboards, files, and tool credentials are read from and written to your own database.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Your account, login, and billing are unaffected by this setting either way — they always stay with Orchid's central identity service, regardless of where your content is hosted.
</Note>
